All about the project

Why do we make this?

Caring for the Earth is not only a policy goal but also a human responsibility. SDG 15: Life on Land calls on us to protect forests, restore ecosystems, and halt biodiversity loss (UN n.d.), yet this mission can only truly matter when we feel connected to the living world around us. As Naskar (2021) writes, “If we don't have a place for nature in our heart, how can we expect nature to have a place for us.” This quote reminds us that nature can only survive in our world when it first exists in our values, choices, and everyday spaces. Give ecosystems a place in your heart by making room for them in your everyday living space.

Idea

Urbanization is one of the reasons for terrestrial ecosystem downgrading (ref). We cant avoid urbanization, but can avoid its destruction just by small actions in our daily life. And by fighting city development consequences, we are leaving more spaces for the ecosystem to cycle and be alive along with us.

Target audience

Vietnamese gen Z living in big cities.

Style guide

We believe using a style with a combination of colorfulness with free-style line-arts will be eye-catching to our target audience, as there have been successful cases on attract young customers using bright and colorful style in branding, for example are Oatside and Vinamilk (Luong 2024, One Republic n.d.).

My individual idea

Urban flooding in Vietnam’s big cities is a growing problem, often caused by clogged drainage systems that become polluted and unhygienic due to littering. These drainage ditches play a vital role in keeping cities safe from excess water (Le et al. 2025), yet when blocked, their flood not only create smelly and unhealthy conditions for residents (Nguyen et al. 2023) but also damage local ecosystems and contribute to land degradation (Zhang et al., 2024). Each person can make a difference by simply avoiding the habit of throwing trash into drainage areas, helping to keep the system clear and functional. Preventing floods is not just about infrastructure, it’s about collective responsibility and everyday actions that protect both communities and the environment.

I took the visual inspiration from the Te canal in district 7 HCMC, where its often get flooded every time high tide happens (Anh Bao 2025).

Instead of the realistic illustration of trash in the canal, I want to depict it as bacterial particles, following team’s style guide.

Instead of showing the actual action, I want to focus on the thoughts, which directly drives the action (littering into the canal). Each click on the falling thoughts will tear it, and as each thought is eliminated, the bacteria in the canal appears less. This idea is to encourage people to aware about the popular thoughts about litter that they, or people they know, might have, and that those thoughts are not appropriate and could lead to actual action.

Despite not directly mentioning about littering, also do not what to leave out the visualization for actual rubbish, since it is the main cause of canal clogging (Ngoc Quynh 2026). The litter is accessed through the fishing man.

I also want to include the busyness and a huge amount of residency (lots of houses and vehicles moving) in order to show that this is a big city, as urban living reduces biodiversity awareness (Nguyen et al. 2023).
